My arts and crafts mediums:

Colouring

In art, "medium" refers to the substance the artist uses to create a piece of artwork. In other words: pens and papers etc.

Colours:

I use an assortment of different colouring pencils, watercolour pencils, pens, alcohol markers and gel pens.
My favourites and most used are Prismacolor Premier they are a bit pricey but divine. A cheaper alternative would be my Derwent coloursoft.

Papers:

I mostly use adult colouring books, sometimes kids colouring books. But I have several sketchpads for my sketches (these won't be available to see here).
My most used books are the ones by Hanna Karlzon and CAMILLA d'ERICO, these amazing artist needs more attention.

Blending:

Blending is used to make the colours seamless join together. There are several ways to do this.
The way I prefer is blending solvent or a specific blending pen. You can use baby or any kind of oils too, but it's not as easy and might leave a stain.

Jewellery:

I have tons of materials for all different kinds of jewellery. Bracelets are my favourites, but earrings can be a lot of fun too.
I went to a goldsmith to learn to do queen- and king chains properly. It takes several hours for each bracelet, but the results are gorgeous.
I also make bead bracelets with all kinds of beads, plastic, gemstones, metal, wood, and letters. My favourites are my curse bracelets.

Soap:

I use a melt and pour kind of soap, as I don't think I should be allowed to play with chemicals.
Then I use one of 10 colours to get the one I want, and lastly I add my scents. I have about 30 different essential oils to always get it right.
